Kevin Choi

Q & A

Q. You entered the university in 2014, why are you still there?

I took a three years break to finish my required military service to the Republic of Korea Army.

Q. I thought you had to serve for only 21 months?

I had a difficulty finding a position in the Army because I was in the 'baby boomer' generation of South Korea which resulted in narrower openings. Also, I needed time to get all the necessary documents for the application when I went back to Korea because I was not able to do so in the U.S. As a result, it almost took me half a year to find the position which enlongated the break.

Q. Ok, so what did you in between?

I worked part time jobs at a cinema and a bar. I would like to say I did it to get some valuable life experiences and I actually did, but the purpose back then was to get some extra cash to spend before the military service. After the service, I worked as an assistant for a small company in my hometown for about half a year.

Q. Why? Couldn't you have returned to school?

I could, but I was not sure about what to do with my life back then so I hesitated coming back to the U.S.

Q. Still the numbers don't add up. Then you should have had returned to school in 2018 and graduated in 2020.

The reason why I hesitated coming back to the U.S. was related to the answer for this question. It is embarassing to say this, but I failed most of the classes in my freshmen year and as a result, I was dropped out of the university. In order to be readmitted, I had to take classes from another institution with good grades as an evidence for an improvement, so I attended the community college nearby the university (Parkland College) from Spring 2018 even though I came back to the U.S. in Summer 2018 (I took online classes while working). Thankfully, I was able to get good grades at there (I got straight A's) and I got readmitted in Summer 2019.

Q. What had been different? Why did you fail in freshmen year but did well in community college?

The major difference was that I was diagnosed with ADHD and depression for the first time in my life while I was attending the community college. I always knew that I was not having the best mental health but diagnosed with and treated for such conditions had more impact than I thought. Plus, the classes from the community college were easier than the equivalents from the university.

Q. Enough about the academics. Why don't you have any experience then?

As you can see from the list above, I took the introductory programming class after the readmission because the community college that I attended did not provide an equivalent class. As a result, now that I think of it, I could have applied for some internships with the knowledges I got back then, but I did not because I was not confident about my skills.

Q. Why would I (my company) hire you? You got subpar grades and no experience.

I agree with you but I think I have some qualities that other applicants might not have. Having gone through several turmoils in my life accompanied by some ups and extreme downs, I was able to find out that the life did not depend on the ups and downs but the attitude I had when I faced them. I was able to figure out that I was the only one who could drag myself out of the holes created by my wrongful decisions and staying in the holes doing nothing but complaining would not change anything. I know that "dragging myself out" takes a hard and honest work while complying with the rules set by the groups that I belong to. Even though I do not have any experience in tech industry, I met and had worked with a broad spectrum of people working in different industries. In short, I would say that I am a bit mature kid who would get the same salary as the younger kids.
