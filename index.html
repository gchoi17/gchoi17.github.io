Q & A

Q. You entered the university in 2014, why are you still there?

A. I took a three years break to fulfill my required military service to the Republic of Korea Army.

Q. I thought you had to serve for 21 months?

A. I had a difficulty finding a position in the Army because I was in the "baby boomer" generation in South Korea and it brought unexpected competitions to enlist. As a result, it almost took me half a year to find an opening which enlongated the duration of the break.

Q. What did you in between?

A. I worked part time jobs at a cinema and a bar. After discharged, I worked as an assistant for a small company in my hometown for about half a year.

Q. Couldn't you have returned to school?

A. I could, but I was not sure about what to do with my life back then so I hesitated coming back to the U.S.

Q. Still the numbers don't add up.

A. I failed most of the classes in my freshmen year and consequently, I was dropped out of the university. In order to be readmitted, I had to take classes from another institution with good grades as an evidence for an improvement, so I attended the community college nearby the university (Parkland College) from Spring 2018 and I came back to the U.S. in Summer 2018. I ended up getting good grades (straight A's) and I got readmitted in Summer 2019.

Q. What had been different from your freshmen year?

A. I was diagnosed with and treated for ADHD and depression while I was attending the community college and it brought a noticeable improvement on my academic performance. Plus, the classes from the community college were easier than the equivalents from the university.

Q. Why don't you have any experience?

A. I had to take the introductory programming class after the readmission because the community college did not provide an equivalent class. Now that I think of it, I could have applied for some internships with the knowledges I got back then, but I did not because I was not confident about my skills. I will be starting my first work experience as a teaching assistant next semester.

Q. Why would my company hire you?

A. I have some qualities that other applicants might not have. I had some serious struggles in my life and I figured out that the life depended more on the attitude I had than the actual ups and downs. I found out I must be the one to drag myself out of the holes created by my wrongful decisions. I know "dragging out" takes a hard work so I better comply with the rules set by the groups that I belong to. I met and had worked with a broad spectrum of people in different industries and I believe it helped me grow as a person.
